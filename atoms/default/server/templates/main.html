“Covid lit a fire under us”: Four businesses bringing new energy into 2021

Australian businesses have a lot to look forward to in 2021. Meet the brands taking what they’ve learned through Covid and turning it into success.

Hear Xavier on how listening to customer insights and trusting the team to make things that help develop good financial habits.

Bringing community spirit back to retail - Nimble Activewear founder Vera Yan and senior marketing manager Justine Cusack

Nimble calls itself “a step towards self-expression for women to celebrate individuality, acknowledge differences and develop self-acceptance.” In 2020, the activewear brand lived those values. This year, the celebration will continue at a rapid pace.

“2020 was so much about agility and moving to what the world and circumstances demanded,” says Nimble co-founder Vera Yan. “2021, for us, is about being a lot more intentional.”

“Community has always been where we build credibility and trust.” - Vera Yan

Senior marketing manager, Justine Cusack, says: “Retail is a big focus. We’ll be opening a new space in May. Before Covid, community was a really big part of our stores … [they were] little community hubs where our customers could interact with our brand. It’s about refocusing there.”

Nimble’s goal is to capture that same community spirit in its online spaces, too. Yan says: “Community has always been where we build credibility and trust. Connecting with our local community and bringing people together has always been a big part of our brand. We’re looking at how we can really tailor the journey to our customer and give her what she’s looking for. A big focus for us is how can we make our online presence more than just a shopfront?”

Yan says Nimble’s goal is to create a consistent experience across all touchpoints, from social media to the website to emails to in-store. As a largely digital business, it aims to leverage technology and make it work harder.

Ultimately, the Nimble team hopes to keep bringing women together to get active. Cusack says: “It's about how that makes you feel, makes you breathe, makes you happy. It’s encouraging people to get out there and move.”
